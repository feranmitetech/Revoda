How to write a strong report
1
Be specific about location
Include the polling unit code if possible. At minimum, include state and LGA.
2
Include exact timing
Note the time the incident occurred, not just when you're reporting it.
3
Describe the impact
How many voters were affected? Was voting disrupted? Were officials involved?
4
Attach evidence if possible
Photos, videos, and screenshots significantly speed up verification.
5
Stay factual
Only report what you directly witnessed or can verify. False reports harm democracy.
⚠ Important: Submitting false or misleading reports is a violation of Revoda's terms and may result in your organisation's account being suspended.
